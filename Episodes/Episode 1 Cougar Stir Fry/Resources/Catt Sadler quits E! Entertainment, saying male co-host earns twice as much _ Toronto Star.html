Catt Sadler quits E! Entertainment, saying male co-host earns twice as much

Sadler stepped down from the network where she spent 12 years after the company refused to pay her comparably to her male co-host, Jason Kennedy.

By MAGGIE ASTORThe New York Times
Thu., Dec. 21, 2017

Catt Sadler said it was her dream job. After 12 years at E! Entertainment, she was a co-host of two shows, Daily Pop during the day and the networks flagship E! News at night.

But on Tuesday, she signed off for the last time because, she said, the company was paying her half as much as her male E! News co-host, Jason Kennedy.

Neither Sadler nor E! would disclose specific salary figures. Sadler said in an interview on Wednesday that in early 2017, a friend who is an executive at the network informed her of the disparity.

Once it came time to negotiate a new contract, she demanded to be paid comparably.

When the company refused, she decided she could not stay, she said.

Its heartbreaking in one sense, but I believe that you have to act in alignment with your beliefs, Sadler said. As much as I wanted to stay, I do know my worth, I do know the inner workings of the network, and I just wanted what was fair and reasonable.

The network, which is owned by NBC, rejected the allegation that it paid Sadler less because she is a woman, saying in a statement that it compensates employees fairly and appropriately based on their roles, regardless of gender.

An E! spokeswoman said Sadlers and Kennedys roles were not comparable.

In addition to co-hosting E! News five nights a week, Kennedy co-hosts Live From the Red Carpet, one of the networks most lucrative programs, while Sadler was one of three hosts of Daily Pop and co-hosted E! News twice a week, the spokeswoman said. (Live From the Red Carpet airs before major awards shows like the Academy Awards and the Grammy Awards, not year-round.)

Sadler said she had taken on substantial new responsibilities when Daily Pop was introduced. For several months, she co-hosted both Daily Pop and E! News five days a week. She eventually cut back on her E! News duties because of the strain of doing both every day. Even after that, she said, she was hosting seven shows a week to Kennedys five.

I inherited a lot more work and several more work hours, and I did all of that all year long without a single extra dime, she said. I did that in good faith because Im a team player and I wanted both shows to succeed. I trusted that, come time to renegotiate, I would be compensated fairly for all of that work moving forward.

Kennedys representatives did not respond to phone calls on Wednesday. Before Sadlers final show, he tweeted that he would miss her but did not mention the pay issue.

After Sadler posted an explanation of her decision online, she received an outpouring of support, including from the actresses Jessica Chastain and Olivia Munn, and the former E! News host Maria Menounos.

I know this wasnt easy, Menounos wrote. Good for you.

On average, American women make 80 cents for every dollar men make. Just two months ago, in a case similar to Sadlers, the Australian television host Lisa Wilkinson quit her job after unsuccessful contract negotiations. Australian newspapers reported that her co-host, Karl Stefanovic, was paid nearly twice as much.

Sadler said she hoped her decision would encourage other women to demand equal pay. As for her own future, she said she would love to host a show that would foster discussions about this very topic.

Acknowledging the support of Chastain, Munn and others, she said, If these women celebrities and non-celebrities alike want to have these conversations on a huge scale for the world to watch and there can be some element of change that comes as a response to that, then thats the kind of TV I would love to make.
